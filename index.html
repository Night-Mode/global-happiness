Global Happiness: Visualizing Trends and Contributing Factors

Happiness in Numbers: A Global Exploration

Purpose

This web page presents an interactive dashboard that explores the statistics driving global happiness. Using a series of visualizations, users can examine how different social, economic, and health-related factors contribute to happiness scores across countries. The project utilizes data from reputable sources such as the World Happiness Report, World Health Organization, Global Health Security Index, and others to quantify and interpret global happiness through various explanatory variables.

Approach

The foundation of our analysis is the 2024 World Happiness Report, which provides ladder scores and key explanatory variables. To expand our understanding, we integrated additional indicators—from digital access to crime rates—to explore their potential relationships with national happiness levels. These ideas are explored and visualized throughout the website using libraries like JavaScript's D3 tools. D3 enables scientists to create detailed and visually appealing representations of complex relationships between data attributes, helping to uncover factors that may influence key outcomes. In this case, visualizations such as choropleth maps, stacked bar charts, spider plots, and box plots support the idea that a country's economic and demographic characteristics can significantly impact the overall happiness of its society.

Data Sources

  • World Happiness Report 2024
  • ICT Development Index (ITU 2024)
  • Global Health Security Index (GHS 2021)
  • Learning-Adjusted Years of Schooling (World Bank 2024)
  • Crime Rates (UNODC 2024)
  • Gini Coefficient for Income Inequality (World Bank 2024)
  • GeoJSON Country Polygons

These datasets were cleaned and merged to support cross-variable comparison and interactive analysis. The full source code and preprocessing steps can be found on our GitHub repository.
